HOW TO LEARN DEUTSCH?

  • GERMAN GRAMMAR

    The only way to learn and improve English grammar is through practice. You need to speak and write as much as possible to avoid common mistakes and familiarize yourself with sentence structure and grammar rules. You can also start reading various materials in English to get used to how sentences are built.

  • READING IN GERMAN LANGUAGE

    Reading is one of the first skills to master on the way to becoming fluent in German. You need to expand your library by connecting a variety of resources such as glossaries, press, blogs, and scholarly journals. Important: do not just read, but also try to take notes and check the meaning and pronunciation of words that seem unfamiliar.

  • SPOKEN GERMAN

    You can improve your German not only by working on grammar and writing, but also by actually speaking words and making sentences. Yes, you will make mistakes, but that's okay. You can start with conversation clubs or find a native speaker to help you correct your mistakes.

  • LISTENING IN GERMAN

    Even if you have a large vocabulary, you should still work on your listening. Most likely, at first you will panic, trying to understand everything that the native speaker is saying. But over time, his speech will begin to "slow down" to an understandable pace. Eventually you will be able to understand German easily.

  • GERMAN VOCABULARY

    The variety of your vocabulary shows your level of knowledge of the German language. Learning new words and phrases helps to enrich your vocabulary faster. If you want to sound natural and free, you need to learn different types of slang - words that go together. You should also consume content in German to better master the use of these expressions.

  • GERMAN PRONUNCIATION

    Understanding the phonetics and pronunciation of German is a challenge for most students. But still, you can finish this topic if you devote enough time and effort. The main goal of correct German pronunciation is to familiarize yourself with the most common rules and use them confidently. As soon as you manage to do this, you will be one step closer to fluent German.
